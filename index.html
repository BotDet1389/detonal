Joanne Rowling ( Eng. Joanne Rowling ; b. July 31, 1965 [6] ), known under the pseudonyms J.K. Rowling (JK Rowling) [7] , Joanne Kathleen Rowling ( Eng. Joanne Kathleen Rowling ) and Robert Galbraith (Robert Galbraith ) , is a British writer , screenwriter and film producer , best known as the author of the Harry Potter series of novels . The Harry Potter books have received several awards and have sold over 500 million copies [8]. They became the best-selling book series in history [9] and the basis for a film series that became the third highest-grossing film series in history [10] . J. K. Rowling herself approved the film scripts [11] and also joined the producers of the last two parts [12] . Rowling was working as a research assistant and secretary-translator for Amnesty International when she had the idea for a Harry Potter novel while traveling by train from Manchester to London in 1990 [13] . In the next seven years, Rowling's mother died, she herself divorced her first husband, and lived in poverty until she published the first novel in the series, Harry Potter and the Philosopher 's Stone (1997). Subsequently, she wrote 6 sequels - as well as 3 additions to this series. Following this, Rowling parted ways with her agency and began writing for adult readers, releasing the tragicomedy Random Vacancy (2012) and (under the pseudonym "Robert Galbraith") crime novels: "Call of the Cuckoo " (2013), " Silkworm " (2014), " In the Service of Evil" (2015), " Deadly White " (2018) and " Bad Blood " (2020). In five years, Rowling went from living on welfare to multimillionaire status . She is the best selling author in the UK, with sales of over £238 million [14] . In 2008, the Sunday Times Rich List estimated Rowling's net worth at £560 million, placing her 12th on the list of the richest women in the UK [15] . Forbes in 2007 ranked Rowling as the 48th most influential celebrity [16] and Time magazine in 2007 gave her second place in the " Person of the Year " award, noting the social, moral and political inspiration she gave to her fans [17]. In October 2010, Rowling was named "the most powerful woman in Britain" by the editors of leading magazines [18] . She became a notable philanthropist, supporting such organizations as Laughter Relief , Gingerbread , Multiple Sclerosis Society of Great Britain , and Lumos ( formerly Children's High Level Group ) .
